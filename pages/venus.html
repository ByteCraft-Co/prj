Venus

Earth's “sister planet” - but much hotter.

Image: Venus

Overview

Venus is a rocky planet with a thick atmosphere mostly made of carbon dioxide. This causes a strong greenhouse effect, making Venus the hottest planet.

Fun facts

  • Venus rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction to most planets.
  • It can be seen from Earth as the “Morning Star” or “Evening Star.”
  • Its clouds contain sulfuric acid.
